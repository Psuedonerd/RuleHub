# Model Explanation: CircadianOscillator

## One-sentence summary

CircadianOscillator demonstrates circadian rhythm.

## What the model shows

CircadianOscillator shows circadian rhythm by focusing on activation promoter binding. The tutorial connects that event to readouts that make the modeled behavior visible.

## Biological story

CircadianOscillator is a tutorial model centered on circadian rhythm. Its narrative moves from activation promoter binding to repressor activator binding, so the model teaches a specific mechanism rather than a broad pathway survey.

## Main biological players

A, R, pA, pR, mRNA_A, mRNA_R

CircadianOscillator uses A, R, pA, pR, mRNA_A, mRNA_R as the main actors. First, activation promoter binding. Next, repressor activator binding. Finally, a degrades unless bound to dna, which is what links the mechanism to the plotted readouts.

- Repressor Activator binding.

## What the model measures

The readouts track Afree, Rfree, Atot, Rtot. These measurements are useful for seeing whether activation promoter binding changes the amount of free, bound, active, inactive, product-like, or complexed material.

## Expected behavior in plots

For CircadianOscillator, compare readouts connected to activation promoter binding with readouts connected to repressor activator binding. Separation between those curves indicates that the tutorial mechanism is changing the system in stages rather than all at once.

## Caveats

CircadianOscillator is scoped to circadian rhythm. It should be read as a focused tutorial or validation example, not as a comprehensive biological pathway model.
