# Model Explanation: rec_dim

## One-sentence summary

rec_dim demonstrates ligand receptor binding.

## What the model shows

rec_dim shows ligand receptor binding by focusing on ligand receptor binding. The tutorial connects that event to readouts that make the modeled behavior visible.

## Biological story

rec_dim is a validation model centered on ligand receptor binding. Its narrative moves from ligand receptor binding to ligand and receptor species bind and separate to control complex abundance, so the model teaches a specific mechanism rather than a broad pathway survey.

## Main biological players

Lig, Rec

## Mechanism in plain English

rec_dim uses Lig, Rec as the main actors. First, ligand receptor binding. Next, ligand and receptor species bind and separate to control complex abundance. Finally, dimer or oligomer formation changes the amount of larger receptor or protein assemblies, which is what links the mechanism to the plotted readouts.

## Key modeled events

- Ligand receptor binding.
- Ligand and receptor species bind and separate to control complex abundance.
- Dimer or oligomer formation changes the amount of larger receptor or protein assemblies.

## What the model measures

The readouts track RecFree, RecBound, Dimers. These measurements are useful for seeing whether ligand receptor binding changes the amount of free, bound, active, inactive, product-like, or complexed material.

## Expected behavior in plots

For rec_dim, compare readouts connected to ligand receptor binding with readouts connected to ligand and receptor species bind and separate to control complex abundance. Separation between those curves indicates that the tutorial mechanism is changing the system in stages rather than all at once.

## Caveats

rec_dim is scoped to ligand receptor binding. It should be read as a focused tutorial or validation example, not as a comprehensive biological pathway model.
