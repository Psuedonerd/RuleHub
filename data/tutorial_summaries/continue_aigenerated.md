# Model Explanation: continue

## One-sentence summary

continue demonstrates test trajectory continuation.

## What the model shows

continue shows test trajectory continuation by focusing on a synthesizes b. The tutorial connects that event to readouts that make the modeled behavior visible.

## Biological story

continue is a validation model centered on test trajectory continuation. Its narrative moves from a synthesizes b to degradation of free b, so the model teaches a specific mechanism rather than a broad pathway survey.

## Main biological players

A, B, C, Trash

## Mechanism in plain English

continue uses A, B, C, Trash as the main actors. First, a synthesizes b. Next, degradation of free b. Finally, b c binding, which is what links the mechanism to the plotted readouts.

## Key modeled events

- A synthesizes B.
- Degradation of free B.
- B C binding.

## What the model measures

The readouts track Atot, Btot, Ctot, Bfree, Cfree, BC. These measurements are useful for seeing whether a synthesizes b changes the amount of free, bound, active, inactive, product-like, or complexed material.

## Expected behavior in plots

For continue, compare readouts connected to a synthesizes b with readouts connected to degradation of free b. Separation between those curves indicates that the tutorial mechanism is changing the system in stages rather than all at once.

## Caveats

continue is scoped to test trajectory continuation. It should be read as a focused tutorial or validation example, not as a comprehensive biological pathway model.
