# Model Explanation: Creamer 2012

## One-sentence summary

Creamer 2012 demonstrates initial values.

## What the model shows

Creamer 2012 shows initial values by focusing on egf binding to an egfr monomer. The tutorial connects that event to readouts that make the modeled behavior visible.

## Biological story

Creamer 2012 is a tutorial model centered on initial values. Its narrative moves from egf binding to an egfr monomer to hrg binding to an erbb3 monomer, so the model teaches a specific mechanism rather than a broad pathway survey.

## Main biological players

EGF, HRG, EGFR, ErbB2, ErbB3, ErbB4, p52Shc1, Grb2, Sos1, Gab1

## Mechanism in plain English

Creamer 2012 uses EGF, HRG, EGFR, ErbB2, ErbB3, ErbB4, p52Shc1, Grb2, Sos1, Gab1 as the main actors. First, egf binding to an egfr monomer. Next, hrg binding to an erbb3 monomer. Finally, hrg binding to an erbb4 monomer, which is what links the mechanism to the plotted readouts.

- HRG binding to an ErbB3 monomer.

## What the model measures

The readouts track EGFREGFR, EGFRErbB2, EGFRErbB3, EGFRErbB4, ErbB2ErbB2, ErbB2ErbB3, ErbB2ErbB4, ErbB3ErbB3. These measurements are useful for seeing whether egf binding to an egfr monomer changes the amount of free, bound, active, inactive, product-like, or complexed material.

## Expected behavior in plots

For Creamer 2012, compare readouts connected to egf binding to an egfr monomer with readouts connected to hrg binding to an erbb3 monomer. Separation between those curves indicates that the tutorial mechanism is changing the system in stages rather than all at once.

## Caveats

Creamer 2012 is scoped to initial values. It should be read as a focused tutorial or validation example, not as a comprehensive biological pathway model.
